On-line investing/trading can be very rewarding as long as you just don't open an account to see how you'll do. Most people just "give it a try".... what you really need to do is learn first.... ignorance in this game can be very expensive.

#1: Learn the basics. Read a few books on the area you're interested in.
Start with: Stock Investing For Dummies
#2: Don't try penny stocks until you've been investing successfully for 3-5 years (if at all).
Most traders won't go near penny stocks because the risk is ridiculous.
#3: Job number one is risk management. The goal #1 to lose less money (contrary to trying to make a lot of money)... if you manage your risk... the winners will take you the rest of the way.... However... if you're losing stocks are more $$$ than the winning stocks.... you're out of business.
#4: Good traders can be "right" only 40% of the time. It's the risk/reward ratio that makes the difference.
#5: Never (ever) take stock tips (from the web, TV, Radio, friends, strangers, "experts").

The market is not a "big gamble". When you lose a lot of money... it's totally your fault... not the "market". If you can't accept that..... don't bother investing or trading. You control your position size.... you control your protective stops. Learn this stuff & you'll do well.

PS: Never use the following words when it comes to your trading;
Hope, wish, believe, expect & the worst phrase is;
"It can't go any lower".....................................
