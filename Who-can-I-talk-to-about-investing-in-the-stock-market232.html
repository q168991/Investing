Read. Read a lot. Take your time.

You have two basic choices;
A. Buy Stock Mutual Funds
B. Buy individual stocks.

I generally believe that buying Stock Mutual Funds is a better idea for most. Less time learning & less time needed to follow your investment. I do both (Stock Funds and individual stocks). I started individual stocks a decade after doing Stock Mutual Funds.

Start your reading with;
Investing For Dummies
Mutual Fund Investing For Dummies
Stock Investing For Dummies

Then read some more books in the area you want to go first.

Remember always;
A. Don't put all your money into one single investment.
B. Stock investing for most requires a 5-10+ year time horizon.
C. Don't use stocks as your savings account. Don't use money you'll need next month, next year or in the next five or more years.
D. Have an "Emergency Fund" in a savings account. There is nothing worse than watching your stocks decline by 30% and have to cash them in for some "unexpected" emergency. There will always be an "unexpected" emergency. Plan for it (that's what adults do).
