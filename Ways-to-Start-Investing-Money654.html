Ways to Start Investing Money?

I am going to High School and earning about $400 a week in a casual job. Could someone please suggest some ways I could invest this money?

Start with whatever lump sum you have, at least $500-1000.

Find a good online broker or open an account at a local brokerage firm with a broker you feel comfortable with.

Buy either Investor's Business Daily or the Wall Street Journal at least twice a week and follow the news in the business and investing community.

Put your first $1500 to work into a stock of your own choosing that you think has the potential to make substantial profits and earnings in the future. At least $300 a month or so is a good start to put into a brokerage account after your inital deposit. After you have a position in one company and sufficient capital to continue investing then look to buy a second stock. When you have over $5000 in stock assets, consider diversification and buying a third stock; watch all your stocks like a hawk, without being overly quick or overly slow to take profits or cut losses. In time, alot of it should come naturally if you continue to educate yourself, read and stay on top of the financial news.

Putting money into a 401K doesn't mean you're doing it correctly. My vote is to not do a thing until you learn the basics. The most logical place to start is Stock Mutual Funds. Get some basic books on Mutual Funds (at least 2-3). This is not going to be painful. In addition also read 2-3 books on stock investing. Then... you'll be ready to choose a broker or no load, low fee Mutual Fund company. I'm not a big fan of eTrade.... but you could do worse. Check out TD Ameritrade, Charles Schwab & Fidelity.
