I wouldn't jump right in to it. Research the issues first and be certain that any money invested is money that you can afford to lose. Remember, all investments in the market are not winners. Some lose big time. First hand, you should have 3-6 months of funds available in an accessible account for emergency funds. Don't ever go without this if you're going to invest in the market as that rainy day could be just around the corner. Literature from brokerage houses or the NYSE could be a place to start learning.

Try Scottrade or TdAmeritrade. I think there is a $1000 minimum.

Become familiar with the online resources like Yahoo Finance. When you see an interesting stock, put it in a Portfolio. Keep notes, possibly in a spreadsheet, about why you like it and what signals you would use to sell. I usually buy 1 share and enter the date and price.

Motley Fool as portfolios for people like Jim Cramer. They have names like "TrackCramer". I think the one I liked was TrackHeebner.

Every library has stock market books.

Open an account with a discount broker. Your bank is no friend of yours. Talk face to face with a discount broker near your home.
