It is great that you will have no credit card or auto loan debt so you can start civilian life with $8,000 on the plus side. Investing involves risk or it is not investing. There are no investments that will give you a "good return" without risking your investment. Also, investing is usually for a longer term than 1 year. Even if someone could identify a good investment (and they really can't) interest rates, the economy and world events can have significant effect on investments especially in the short term.

I don't know what your situation will be when you get out but jobs are hard to find and you won't have your room and board etc paid by the military. So, you might want to consider making sure that $8,000 isn't at much risk, and in fact, try to add to it. Otherwise you could find when you get out that $8,000 that was invested is worth $6,000 and you don't have a job and then you could undo the no debt situation.

When savings rates are so low people are looking to get more return. That usually means they take more risk, even though sometimes they don't realize it. There are lots of companies that know this and create ads or products they play on this "greed". When savings rates are low and the stock market (or real estate market, or gold etc) is good very conservative people decide to "invest". Of course they usually invest just when the market is high and then they have a problem.

If you want the money in a year you should put it into savings even if the returns are low. That way the money you have will be there when you need it. After you adjust to civilian life, job, housing etc then you might consider investing some of your nest egg. For savings I suggest looking at Ally Bank or Discover Bank. They usually have the best of the low rates. Ally Bank has a no penalty CD that if you need the money you can get it without paying a penalty. Most CDs have a 3 or 6 month interest penalty if you cash in a CD early (Ally has a 2 month penalty on their regular CDs).

You might also look at what the military offers. Be careful to make sure your savings are insured by the FDIC or the similar government insurance for credit unions and that there will be no or low penalty for cashing it in early. Beware of ads or pitches that tempt you to buy something with much higher rates. There is almost always a catch. If safe rates are 1% there is no way there is a safe product that offers 3% without a catch.
