eTrade has "hidden fees" like inactivity. Of the two Scottrade is the better choice on many levels. Both are mediocre brokers.... but Scottrade is (as I said)... the better of the two.

Picking a broker based on price or popularity is the wrong way to go about it. You should base it on what you need. You won't know what you need until you decide what type of investing/trading you'll be doing. The best way to know this is to read a few books and get a feel for what you'll need. It would be sad to open an account with a broker & want to do trailing stops or OCO orders and find out you can't do them. Maybe you'll want to do a MOC order. Or some other type. Maybe you need a good charting package on the platform. Maybe you'll need a software "platform" instead of a web based based platform.

Maybe you'll be better off just opening a Mutual Fund. Or a DRIP.

Good luck.
