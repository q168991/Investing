an emergency fund, by definition, is a very liquid asset that does not lose value. cash in a savings account is one of the best. next would be a short term CD, but you could get an early withdrawal penalty if you end up needing it before maturity. everything else is an investment that is riskier that could lose value.

There is no question that savings accounts barely pay any interest but the advantage is liquidity. You can go to an ATM and make a withdrawal immediately. So it is a trade off. I would suggest a combination approach. Put a month or two into a savings account available for immediate withdrawal and invest the remainder in an Exchange Traded Fund (ETF) that is indexed to a market such as the S & P 500 instead of a mutual fund. An ETF is very liquid and allows you to sell immediately. Understand that there is no guarantee that the S &P will go up but most ETF's do earn interest based on thew underlying stocks. Set up an online brokerage account that does not charge maintenance fees and does not require minimum balances.
