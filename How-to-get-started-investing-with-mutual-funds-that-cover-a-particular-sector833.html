A brokerage account may not be the best thing for you. Generally, you'll have to pay brokerage fees each time you buy or redeem mutual fund shares. Pick a good mutual fund company like Vanguard or T. Rowe Price and buy the funds directly from them with no fees. Both of those have many fund choices, including equity sector funds.

E-trade you can always buy ETF's which are mutual funds that trade like stocks. My favorite play is Vanguard however also look into Wisdomtree, Rydex. Vanguard does have a tech ETF called Vanguard Information Technology with core holdings in Microsoft, Apple, IBM, Cisco Systems, Google, Intel, HP, etc. It's a rock solid tech fund with an expense ratio of only .25%.

Stay away for renewable energy and just go with an all purpose energy fund. With oil prices dropping recently it will be harder to make money in solar, wind, etc. Good luck!

Like some others here, I'm not a big fan of doing mutual funds through brokerage accounts. I prefer to deal directly with the funds themselves.

My favorite fund families are Vanguard, Fidelity, and T. Rowe Price - all have a great selection of funds (including sector funds), low fees, and excellent customer service.

To my knowledge there are no funds that invest exclusively in renewable energy - most energy funds invest in ALL types of energy, including renewables.

Here are some funds that might interest you in each of these families:

VANGUARD: Energy Fund (VGENX); they do not have a technology fund

FIDELITY: Select Computers (FDCPX); Select Materials (FSDPX); Select Software & Computer Services (FSCSX); Select Technology (FSPTX);

T. ROWE PRICE: Media & Telecomm (PRMTX); New Era [Energy/Natural Resources] (PRNEX); Science & Technology (PRSCX)

I hope that helps. Good luck!
