Like anything in life, if you want to learn about it you need to read a book about it.

Also, here's a simple way to buy stocks. Google General Electric Investor Relations, or Johnson and Johnson Investor relations. They will provide links on how you can start direct investment plans. I think both companies start their plans at $250. And you can send in checks to purchase additional shares when you have the money. Both of these stocks pay a dividend every three months, which means for every 1 share you own of Johnson and Johnson, you will receive 55 cents that you can either withdraw (bad idea), or reinvest to purchase more shares at no charge (good idea).

That's a simple way to have your money working for you.

I agree with Ben9999. Read books or online websites like The Motley Fool. The Fool website is also a community were you can read unbiased opinions of all kinds of stocks and other investments. You can also post questions that you might have. It is very useful.
Also Ben mentioned buying stocks direct and re-investing the dividends. These are called "DRIP"-Direct Re Investment Programs. They are very good to get into if you buy into a well established company.
Investopedia*** is another good rescource website for beginners.

As suggested above read up as much as you can on the stock market. In addition to DRIP's by individual companies look into ING's Sharebuilder account. You can buy fractional shares like a DRIP but have them all in a single account. The $4 commission is not cheap, but reasonable as you learn.

Also check into NAIC investment clubs in your area. ***betterinvesting.org. There are some cities that have teenage clubs that have a mentor to help the team learn.

Good Luck! You are wise to learn about the market when you are young.
