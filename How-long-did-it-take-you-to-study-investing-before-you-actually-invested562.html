I was invested in mutual funds for several years prior to taking a fling at individual stocks. During that time, I studied the prospectus of each fund, examining closely each of the top ten holdings within each fund as are commonly listed for all to see. I went to corporate websites and then compared notes defining a particular holding to sister companies that were publicly traded. What I found were glaring discrepancies in terms of performance as evidenced by their balance sheets and managerial styles.

In time I was invited to write for Marketwatch (CBSMarketwatch at that time) and then was picked up by a Houston energy company to head up its investor relations division. I took the latter position as it enabled me to access the tutelage of both the President and Chairman of the company. This has paid off immensely. From there it was sponsorship enabling me to become a certified money-losing adviser (LOL). Truth be told, I don't ever expect to stop learning and frankly, there's much more to be learned than I've gained thus far.

This is the most exciting field for me, no question about it. Every day is a blessing---full of excitement and filled with gains and also some losses---but at least I get to live it my way without apology or resentments or regret.

Len
