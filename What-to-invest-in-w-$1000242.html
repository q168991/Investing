Only a few good answers at 13.

Invest in yourself. Take some music lessons, or some karate lessons, or a class in the stock market. Dont pay for lessons you can get for free, but if you are really interested in something beyond what you can learn at school or teh library or online, investing in yourself is the BEST thing you can do. And you can do it $25 and one book at a time.

Invest in a business. Buy TWO bicycles, one to ride and one to rent. Buy or start a newspaper route. Buy some car detailing equipment/supplies and some business cards and get your butt out there working afternoons and weekends. Buy a lawnmower and leaf blower. These kind of investments should pay off if you invest time as well as invest money.

Invest in another CD. Get the best rate you can, but maybe the answer is to keep adding to your CD until you have more money to invest in something else. Learn about compounding of interest. at 3%, it takes 24 years for your money to double. But if CD rates go to 5% by next year, it will only take 14 years, and at 8% will only take nine years. Patience is a big part of learning to invest, and your comment "not making any money there" tells me that learning patience would be a good thing for you. Maybe the BEST thing.

Because you are 13 and cannot open a broker account, I would stay away from stocks. Shop around your local banks and credit unions and see if anybody has a better rate on a $1000 CD than your bank.

If you don't need the money for several years, gold may be very profitable, the price is quite low right now, and will probably increase due to inflation. Go you your local coin shop and see if they sell Krugerrands, American Eagles or Maple Leaf's. Gold is $734 an ounce right now, but coins are hard to obtain, so they sell at a premium. If you are lucky, they will have one you can afford.

Anyway, if you manage to buy gold, sock it away somewhere, watch gold prices and wait. When you need money, or when gold hits $3000 an ounce, go back to the coin shop and sell them your coin.

Good Luck, young man.
