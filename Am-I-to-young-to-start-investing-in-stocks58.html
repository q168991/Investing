1. a) Am I too young
No. You have three options:
i.) Wait until you're 18, so you can open an account on your own.
ii.) Get one of your parents to open a custodial account in your name.
iii) Get one of your parents to open an ESA 529 Plan (Education Savings plan). Your parents can contribute up to $2000 per year until you're 18 for college. The returns are tax free: http://***irs.gov/taxtopics/tc310.html

1. b) Am I too stupid
No because of what you put in your additional details. The best market participants aim to minimize risk and maximize return. The dumbest only think they will make returns and then show off when they do.
