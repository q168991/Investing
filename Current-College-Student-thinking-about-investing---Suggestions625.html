I'm a big fan of mutual funds, because they provide several advantages over other types of investments:

- Diversification - most funds invest in dozens (sometimes hundreds) of different securities, so you're not "putting all your eggs in one basket."
- Professional management - Usually it's a team of professionals who do all of the research and monitoring of the investments in the portfolio. You can see how good they are at their job by looking at the fund's performance versus other similar funds and/or appropriate indices.
- Convenience - You can get your account balance daily, and in most cases there is no charge to switch between funds. Also, you can set up your fund account to draw automatic monthly investments regularly from your bank account (this is a great way to build up your balance).
- Liquidity - Depending on the type of account (IRA, taxable, etc.), you generally can take out some or all of your money at any time.
- Flexibility - there are thousands of different types of mutual funds for all types of investors. Starting out with an index fund is a good idea, but as you learn more you might want to add different types of funds as well.

If you do go with mutual funds, be sure to invest with a fund company that has a good fund selection, low fees, and excellent customer service. My favorite companies are Vanguard, T. Rowe Price, and Fidelity.

I hope that helps. Good luck!

PS - GREAT idea to get started young. Time is your best friend when it comes to investing. Trust me - you will be very glad you started early!
