You should definitely do an IRA because of tax benefits. With a traditional IRA, you can deduct the amount you invest from your taxes, but have to pay for taxes when you withdraw after you reach retirement. With a Roth IRA, you don't deduct your investments from your taxes, but when you withdraw it after you retire, you don't pay for taxes for your gains.

With your ETF, you have to pay taxes every year on your capital gains. ETF are good for investing and making money, but not as good for retirement.

Of course, if your employer offers a 401(k), do it because you put some of your income into it and not have to pay taxes for that income. Also most companies, puts money into your 401(k) based on how much you invest.

There are four stages of retirement planning. 1. Contribution, 2. Growth, 3. Income, 4. Transfer. Very few advisors discuss the most important stages which are the last two. How is your nest egg going to be taxed when taking retirement income and upon your death how is your nest egg going to transfer to your heirs.
We use a concept called Tax-Free Income Planning. Where you use after tax dollars to fund your retirement account. The growth (which can be tied to an Index providing good returns but more importantly no downside market risk) will grow tax-deferred, your income will be Tax-Free! Upon your death your nest egg will transfer to your heirs with no taxes.
