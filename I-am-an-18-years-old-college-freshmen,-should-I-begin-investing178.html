One of the constant themes of financial textbooks is that compounding gets more and more powerful the longer the time horizon. Consider that $10,000 invested at 11% for 10 years is only $167,220, while the same amount invested for 40 years is roughly $5,818,261 – four times as long results in nearly 35x as much money in your accounts. That is why it is so vitally important for you to start as young as you can; put money into a Roth IRA and always, always, always take the 401k contribution match provided by your employer.

It is more time efficient for you to purchase ETF exchange traded funds or mutual funds rather than individual stocks. You do not have the time or the expertise to learn all that you need to know to invest successfully.

If you want something more than a savings account you must hire a qualified financial adviser to invest for you. Check the credentials for education and licensing and background to create a successful portfolio for you. (You would be amazed at how easy it is to call yourself an investment representative or a financial adviser.)

But please diversify, and do not get greedy.
