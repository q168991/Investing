To truly answer your question I really need to know EXACTY what you want to learn.

If you want to learn about Big Picture (economic cycles) and why history is filled with economic booms and busts then I recommend:

Manias, Panics and Crashes A history of Financial Crises by Charles P. Kindleberger and Robert Z. Aliber

Would you like to learn how to become a technical trader? This is the sort of thing where you will look at moving averages, candle stick patterns and various indicators on stock charts. Then make buy and sell decisions based on these indicators. I would recommend reading this book:

How to get started in Active Trading and Investing by David S. Nassar

If you would like to learn how to trade options. I could recommend two books for beginners:

Getting Started in options By Michael C. Thomsett
Options Made Easy By Guy Cohen.

I am an options trader. It is my opinion, if your goal is to earn modest and consistent profits in the equity markets this in the most effective method. If you read a few books and decide that you have an interest in options trading. If you would like some “Old School” books… (from a time when the only tools traders had were price action, volume and tape reading) although these books are clearly out of date, I believe the careful reader can uncover principals that still apply today; just as they did many years ago. Here is list of a few of my favorites:

Psychology of the Stock Market Classic Edition By G.C. Selden
The Battle for Investment Survival By G.M. Loeb
How to trade in Stocks By Jesse Livermore
Tape Reading and Market Tactics By Humphrey B. Neill

If you would like a “Novel” style book, roughly based on the life and times of Jesse Livermore (arguably the greatest investor in history). Then I would recommend

Reminiscence of a Stock Operator By Edwin Lefèvre.

If you find you have a true passion for investing, you will eventually read all of these books and many more.
