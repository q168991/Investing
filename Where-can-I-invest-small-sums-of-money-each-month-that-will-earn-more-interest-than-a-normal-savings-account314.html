Where can I invest small sums of money each month that will earn more interest than a normal savings account?

I was planning on saving probably $25-$50 per month. I considered savings bonds but it would only get 1.4% and is not really worth the hassle. Any Ideas? Would a DRP program be useful? It isn't practical with money you might need in the next few years.

I have put money in saving bonds -- TIPS Bonds now -- my entire life. I started when I was about your age. I'm 55 now. Forget the current rate. With small amounts of money, that isn't the point. (Think about it -- is 10% of $25 per year a lot of money? No, it is not)

Try a credit union. And open an money market account. It will be fine for your purpose.

I also started investing in DRP's when I was young. I still have one -- ExxonMobil (it was Mobil when I started) -- and it is worth several hundred thousand dollars. But it is worth emphasizing: I never sold any of it and I reinvested every single dividend payment. The dividend -- please also note -- has averaged around 2% on new money for a very long time. But it is much, much higher on all that old money I put in and never took out.

(Stocks pay dividends as "cents per share." If you buy a share for $1 and it pays 2 cents per share, that's a 2% dividend on new money. If the price goes up to $10 a share and the company is still paying about 2%, that's 20 cents a share. But if I still own the $1 share, I get the 20 cents (per share, not per dollar invested). 20 cents is 20% on a $1 purchase price. Pretty darn good return in any day and age)

DRIP programs are great but keep in mind.... this is a long term investment, which can go up or down. While you may expect to make money... you could easily lose 20%, 30% or more depending on the company and the economy.

Check out Electric Orange. They have a program for buying stocks that might suit you.
