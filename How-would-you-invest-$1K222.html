It depends on your financial situation.

If you have revolving debt -- credit card, home equity loan, etc -- pay off the highest interest debt (or at least some of it.. with financial insecurity these days, having some cash on hand can be wise even with debt).

If your debt is under control, then think about what you need from savings. Do you need it to be easily accessible for emergencies? Do you have easily liquidated savings already for day to day issues and so don't need this amount to be as accessible for some time?

If you need it to be available in case you might need it over the next year, then a savings account is probably your best bet. You generally get next to nothing for interest unless it's a considerable sum -- usually $25k or more -- but you have no risk (look for a FDIC insured bank) and can access the money at any time with no penalty.

If you don't need access to it for a long time, then a FDIC-insured CD is also safe and usually has higher interest paid to you; the catch is, you can't touch that money without losing ALL the interest and usually paying a penalty, until the duration of the CD is done. The longer the term, the better the interest you earn. If you know for sure you won't touch it for a year or more -- for example, a teen saving money for college in 3 years -- a CD can be a good option.

Stock is very volatile and thus very risky. You can get higher returns but you can also lose your shirt. My father lost most of my college savings by what he thought was safe investing in the stock market -- it turned out that the stock he bought tanked and went from $10 a share to next to worthless in the three years I had until college. It turned around and became decent again over time, but I needed that money in 3 years, not 10, and had already sold at the huge loss to get what we could when I needed it. It generally is a reasonable investment for very long term funds that you have time and resources -- and knowledge -- to take well-judged risk with; retirement plans, for example (especially with company matching programs).
