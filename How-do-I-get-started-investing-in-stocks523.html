If you don't know too much about stocks and dont know where to start, you might want to consider investing in a mutual fund. A mutual fund is a fund that invests in many stocks and is professionally managed. These funds will most likely give you a much better return than an ING account over the long run. If you want to invest in individual stocks, I think it's a good idea as long as you know what your getting into. You should know the risks involved. I takes a bit more on you part when getting started. You have to know what stocks you want to invest in and why.

Since your dont know much about a stock you may want to buy a mutual fund. Some companies are no load (means no commission) and you can set up a systematic investment. That is invest $100 per month (or more). This way you buy more shares when the mutual fund is cheap and less when expensive.

As you get more comforatble with investments then start looking at stocks. If you want to get started with stock keep in mind the following...

what do people really need (these stocks will probably do well alll the time)

What do people really want (these stocks will probably do well in Christmas or when economy recovers)
