The best answer I can give you is to not put your money in the market just yet. That is great that you want to get involved, however, you need to know that walking into the market with 1000.00 in your hand and no knowledge, would be like jumping into a school of parannah's. Seriously, you should take your money and go buy some books to get schooled up first. There are two distinctly different trading styles, one is called Technical Trading, and the other is called Fundamental trading. Fundamental trading involves all that hard stuff like anaylzing a business's income statement and balance sheets etc to get a feel for its worth. Then after YOU decide how much you think the company is worth, you check the actual market price. If its trading below what you calculated it "should" be worth, then you might be inclined to buy it in hopes its trade price will one day rise upto its calculated value. But, lemme ask you something... do you know how to accurately assign a value to a multimillion dollar company based on its Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Cash Flow and Earnings Forecast? Hell no. Neither do I. :) Some people do, they are Warren Buffet, Peter Lynch, etc. This type of investing is slow as molasas. If you had 1000 dollors to invest, it would take you a whole lifetime to turn it into anything decent buying stuff like the dow jones index. That thing goes up about 10% per year. Think about it... if you put in 1000 dollors for a whole year, you would make 100 bucks (About). Then your trading fee's, 10 to get in, 10 to get out, now you have only made 80 bux. That is a complete waste of your time. Filthy rich people put zillions of dollars in the dow jones and 10 percent on that is ALOT. but not for only 1k.
The OTHER kind of Trading is called Technical Trading. With this method you dont even look at all that mind numbing data. you look at the price trends. What is something worth? As much as someone is willing to pay for it. You understand that right? So, there are some things about the price action on a daily/weekly/monthly basis that give up clues as to how everyone is feeling about the stock. Get you a nice book on technical trading, I like the one called "Technical Analysis Plain and Simple". Dont cry when you see the 40 dollar price tag, consider that your first investment. Then read up. Next, open an Etrade account. Fund it with your money. Then start applying what you learned in the book to the real world.
I would also suggest you have a look at the "Penny Stock" market. These stocks move around very quickly. Its like a time warp and a whole year of price action can take place in a week with these little guys. The big market MIGHT let you eek out 80 bux in a year with 1k. In the penny stock market, I could turn that 1k into 5k on a good week! Thats where you need to begin with small money.

Know this... its brutal. Its like learning to ride a bike. You can read all about it, but when it comes time to actually trade, your going to wreck. and when you get up, your gonna wreck again. and again. Finally you will get the hang of it.... but only if you persist. If you give up after the first crash or two, you will never learn to ride that bike and you will walk away and tell your friends and family about the time you invested in the stock market and lost all your money, and how they should never try either. . Once you learn to ride the bike, then you can start to work on tricks like riding with no hands, or popping wheelies! Same thing here. they say the vast majority of people trying to do this on thier own dont last more than 6 months. they start to break even after than, and dont become profitable until 18months. It all depends on how hard headed you are and how much you are willing to study to learn the ropes. If you think you can gut trade and wing it, your going to crash and burn. If you instead put in the effort, and try hard and keep getting up, you can make it. once you get the hang of it, it is life changing. Good luck sir.
