People have gotten rich purely on bond investments alone but they are also not without risk even though they are contracts (bonds) for a defined return. With any contract, there is always the possibility that the parties simply can not live up to their obligations. The stock market offers the best opportunity for a return short of actual entrepreneurialism, the stock market allows you to share in the benefits of other people's entrepreneurialism. However, you don't have to learn to pick stocks or trade to become rich. Just investing in a no load, low fee index fund beats out half the investors out there so you can simply dollar cost average into an index fund effectively investing in the entire market. You can do a little better by splitting your regular contributions between an index fund and a bond fund, perhaps in a 50/50 split and rebalancing your portfolio annually and or after any major changes in the market. If you are truly risk averse, a portfolio of 25% stock and 75% bonds has the lowest risk, lower than a 100% bond portfolio even though it has higher returns. There are "risk free" investments such as CD's, savings accounts and treasury bonds but they are only risk free because of deposit insurance, the treasury bonds are considered risk free because they are backed by the full force of the US government. The problem with the "risk free" investments are that they rarely keep up with inflation.
