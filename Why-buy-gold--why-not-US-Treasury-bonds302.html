The security of treasury bonds depends upon the creditworthiness of the U.S. government. If it turns out that the US government can't, in fact, pay off its debts (without vast inflation, which would amount to a de facto default, since the dollars they use to pay interest payments would become almost worthless), then gold may turn out to be more secure. For the past 11 years, gold has increased in value a great deal faster than any bonds. But past performance is always a poor guide to future performance. For these reasons, no one really knows for sure whether treasury bonds are more secure than gold--we can't predict the future. In general, people who buy gold are making a bet that our economy is heading for a crisis even greater than the "great recession."

If inflation kicks in, as it must sooner or later, then bond prices could plummet, even if the interest payments continue.

On the other hand, the security of gold depends solely on the willingness of others to pay a price equal to or greater than the price you paid in buying the gold. If a substantial number of gold owners decide that gold is over-valued (just as home buyers in 2008-9 decided that homes were over-valued), then gold prices could plummet.
