Hi Chad,

As a young, minimally capitalized and inexperienced investor the best investment by far, would be in education. The stock market is no place for people who have no idea what they are doing. An analogy would be standing on one side of a busy six lane expressway blindfolded and attempting to cross to the other side to pick up a bag of cash with your name on it. The likelihood of your being flattened is much greater than that of you actually getting across.

But don't despair, learning how to invest intelligently is not particularly difficult. It doesn't have to be confusing although it can be if you let it. The key is to learn how to do just one thing, but become an expert at it.

Be patient. Get prepared. Save your money. The stock market will still be there when you are ready to make it work for you.

Think about it.

Timothy C. Schewe
