Hi Marc,

Like Brad said, I think you meant ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds). ETFs are similar to Mutual Funds, but have much lower "Management" fees (MERs) that you need to pay compared to those of Mutual Funds. The average annual fee for ETFs lies around 0.50% to 0.75% while it is about 2% to 3% or more for Mutual Funds. This makes a HUGE difference in the long term. These are fees that you must pay when you own funds. Moreover, ETFs are ***much*** safer than investing in individual stocks. ETFs are "basket of stocks" in one stock. So you have diversification - which is something extremely important when investing.

I agree with pagodaboy - you should start a Roth IRA with any money you can spare. An IRA is a retirement savings account, where you invest normally and don't have to pay capital gains tax - but you can't have the interest earnings until you're 60. Choosing stocks is very difficult. You need to look at the company's history of dividend payments as well as judge how you think they'll do in the future. Unless you do a ton of research it becomes little more than a game of chance. You should probably start with a mutual fund with a long history of gains, or the simplest choice is an index fund.
