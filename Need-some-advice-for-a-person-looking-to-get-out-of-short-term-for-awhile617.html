Don't get discouraged. Your emotional disarray sounds appropriate to the scenario.

You have a modest knowledge of investing. Yet you have attempted aggressive short term trading, one of the more challenging ways to invest. The results are not surprising.

Consider VTSMX (Vanguard Total Stock Market) or its ETF equivalent. Get 5-10 years of long term investing experience before trying anything short term again.

An investment banking job at Goldman might require 90 hour weeks. The vast majority of investment jobs are more reasonable. Good challenges, great rewards.

Go back through some of your earlier questions. Some of the advice you rejected may make a lot more sense now.

Actually what you did is what many others have done, thinking they can guess the market and make frequent trades like that, and the story is almost always the same. Your best bet is to make longer term investments and scale in / out. Jmo. When you are out of college, my advice is to sign up for actions alerts plus and trade with them, so you get in the right stocks and also learn how they do it.

Obviously you can't panic when down on a stock, because you will always be down on a stock at times because no one can ever pick the absolute bottom.
