I think you're confused about the difference between a stock broker and an investment advisor. I broker just acts on what you tell them to do, or that they can convince you to do. They dont invest your money for you with the intent of beating the market, merely filling their pockets with commissions. They have no fiduciary responsibility, although they may have with the financial reform bill soon. But I dont think it made into the bill. Investment advisors have a fiduciary responsibility to act in their clients best interests. And what they advise depends on the clients risk tolerance and their goals and objectives. Not just beating an index. But investing with someone who has more resources available to them and that you can hold legally responsible seems to me to be a good reason to use one. Assuming you have a large enough portfolio to make the cost worthwhile
