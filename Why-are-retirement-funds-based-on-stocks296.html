Retirement plans offer a variety of options besides the stock market. They have to, by law. Many people are afraid of the stock market and do not understand it. This is why alternatives are made available. You can invest in bonds, money market funds, or guaranteed savings funds.

However no matter where you put your money you are gambling. A lot of people forget that money (cash) is actually just a bunch of paper. This paper is almost guaranteed to lose value over the long term because governments constantly print new cash to finance their deficits. The rate at which money loses its value is called "inflation". You see this in the form of rising healthcare costs, gas prices, and food prices.

Many people have saved "conservatively" (no stocks) their entire lives only to be hit by a sudden surge of inflation right before they retire. They may have expected to have plenty of money to live comfortably in retirement, but with the price of everything rising so quickly, they find themselves suddenly running out.

With stocks you actually become a part owner in a business. This business owns hard assets such as real estate, food processing plants, electric utilities, factories, (whatever the business does). When prices rise due to inflation, these businesses simply raise their prices. Profits increase, and stock prices rise.

The stock market does look like a gamble at first. Afterall there are plenty of day-traders and wall street banks competing with each other for short term gains, sending stock prices in all directions on a day to day basis. However behind these fluctuations are actual growing businesses whose fundamental returns and value cannot be ignored.

It is best to diversify your retirement with a combination of stocks, as well as bonds and cash. Believing that over the long term investing in cash is somehow less of a gamble than the stock market is naive. Remember that the government is always printing money, a gamble on cash is a bet on the Federal Reserve. Historically this hasn't proven itself to be a wise bet. Over the past 100 years, the value of cash has declined by almost 97% due to inflation turning $1.00 into about 3 cents. Simultaneously the stock market gained about 10% annually turning 1 dollar into $13,780 today.

When you are younger and have a very long time before you retire it is best to invest in stocks. As you get older and approach retirement it is generally wise to move more of your savings into bonds and cash so that a sudden short term market crash won't ruin your retirement plans.
