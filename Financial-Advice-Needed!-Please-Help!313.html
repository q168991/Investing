Although you say you do not want the risk of the stock market - I think you should have some money in stock. The market is volitile right now, but has performed well over the long-term.

You need a cash reserve - I would keep about $10,000 in a money market checking account or savings account - check out rates for these at your bank or on line.

You might want to consider a mutli-sector bond fund for some of your investment dollars - since you appear conservative - probably about 50% of assets you would be willing to put some place other than CD's.

For the other 50% you might want to consider a conservative or moderately conservative asset allocation mutual fund.

Because you have a child coming and do not own a home, yet, you really want to invest where there is -0- risk of losing some of your hard-earned savings. Both the stock market and mutual funds stand to lose if the current market declines.
