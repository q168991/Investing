There are many ways to take advantage of the current economic crisis but some may be frowned upon even though they are legal. The main thing is to familiarise yourself with them. So you're Canadian and you're looking from a Canadian point of view, but if you travelled a little to see how some poorer countries work you'll get a different interpretation of ethics.
Now although it's frowned upon in more modern countries, money lending is legal and quite a part of everyday life in many eastern european countries. You offer finance secured on property and actually have the property transferred to your name until the deal is complete. Now that might sound underhand, but when you consider that people actually trade in property here, it's not quite as underhand as it appears. (i.e. someone will give you a flat in exchange for work in many of these countries).
Then there are other ways, and there's a legal term for this which i've forgotten now, there are actually solicitors (i think you call them lawyers!) who are specifically employed to work these things out. I'll give an example: A large food chain in America or Canada may have a deal with a chocolate company in Switzerland such that they are the sole retailer. In other words in exchange for a guaranteed yearly trade the chocolate company will refuse to allow any other company to import their chocolates. However, if you were to set up a company in an eastern european country which has no such deals and you imported from Switzerland to the eastern european country, then exported to the US or Canada, you could distribute to whoever you liked!
It's the law of loopholes! (big money in chocolates by the way!)
