First I commend you on starting so early in life. It will make a big difference. Second, thank you for your service!! Now, you can not really dollar cost average into individual stocks as the prices may not let you buy even number of shares (which you need to do with stocks). You can however dollar cost average into a mutual fund of blue-chip stocks holding the equities you mentioned. In addition, a fund allows more easily adding to principle which is as sound an investment strategy as ever invented. The $6k to "play around" with might be better suited to buy individual stock which are appealing to you based on company fundamentals at that time.
As for an "approach", I admire your savings mentality. I would recommend adding to the ROTH as you can, redirect the TSP to an aggressive equities account, target date or Life Cycle funds are not a great place if you are willing to learn how to save for retirement. They are for the financially challenged and you are clearly not that. Better to use a common stock fund or domestic stock fund split with an international component. Does the TSP offer those things? Its been a while since I worked with a gov't employee. One more thing. Although you are interested in saving for retirement, I would strongly consider putting money away in a second (not your normal) bank. Accumulate 4-5 months of expenses in this 2nd bank. This is to cover the surprises that life throws at you. Without it, you will be tapping retirement accounts and that can be prohibitively expensive in taxes and penalties.

Investing is one of the best and most important ways you can save money for the future.In long term investment, you are giving your money the time it needs to work for you behind the scenes until you're ready to start cashing in on your accomplishments. You must try to consider to understand that even though long-term investing is far safer financially than short-term investing, there is still the chance that you will lose money. The risks are much lower, especially when you choose less-volatile mutual funds or investment opportunities, but they are still there. Markets and investment values will fluctuate up and down, and unless you've made unsound investments, your chances of seeing profits are fairly good with long-term investments.
