I was once in your shoes and I thought it was enough to just put it in a bank account and get a few quarters in interest every month. You have to understand the concept of risk/reward. Stocks might pay you 5% or more, but that's because there is risk involved in holding stocks. There are two kids of stocks to consider- growth and conservative stocks. Growth is more speculative, usually. Think of a company like Apple. You might buy into it, and in 5 years Apple might go out of fashion. However, a more conservative bet would be like Walmart or Chevron... a company that is already well established and is reliable to deliver steady, somewhat boring returns. The good things about most of the conservative stocks is that they pay a dividend. So, for each share you hold, you will get 50 cents or something, which you can have automatically reinvested for free to buy more shares. Those more shares, then get a dividend, which buy more shares and it goes on and on until one day you're rich.

in your case, I would recommend researching direct investment plans. Some require $500 minimum to start up. And you do not want any that charge you for additional share purchases or dividend reinvestment fees. Some that I can recommend are Kelloggs cereal, which you can start into for as little as $100 I think. General Mills and Conocophillips are two other good ones.

Go to each of those company's investor relation websites for more info. But you could set up a Kellogg account for $100 and have one share of the company in about a week. That will at least get your feet wet.

Then you should read some books about stocks. Read books about Warren Buffet. Basically, you need to find good companies and buy them at the right price. Good luck.

If you can risk losing it (at 14 you can most likely) Get one of your parents to set up a custodial account for online trading of stocks. Most let you start with $500. Then spend a good bit of time looking for a few stocks of companies that are fairly strong but have a stock price that has gone down mostly due to everything being down in price. When you find a couple you like that you feel have a strong business but the price doesn't reflect that (it's lower than it should be) then buy those. If you put in the time and effort to research the companies you invest in, and don't get emotional with your trading, you can make quite a bit of money, especially with the stock market being down right now. The key is putting in the research time and paying attention to your investment. Again, only do this if you can risk losing the money.
