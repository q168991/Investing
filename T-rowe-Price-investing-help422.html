Stocks will fluctuate violently in the short run, but tend to produce the best returns over the long haul. 20 years should be plenty of time.

PIMCO total return is a bond fund. This is an alternative to stocks. Bonds pay interest, fluctuate less in price, and tend to earn lower returns over the long run. More conservative investors generally like bonds.

The retirement funds (retirement 2010 for example) are a mix of stocks and bonds. Dates further out have more stocks/less bonds. As the retirement date approaches, the fund changes to hold more bonds. It's a decent theory because most people like to be more defensive as they get older, however by automatically timing your market exit you can switch out at the wrong time.
