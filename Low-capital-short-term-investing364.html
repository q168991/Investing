So the pricing works like this - let's say Silver spot or Gold spot (Spot = the current base price) - silver is currently at $33.180 /oz. To show you exactly how unstable it is, last month it was under $29 /oz. So when you buy silver or gold, companies will charge you spot price ($33.18 silver) + a percentage over spot. It's usually like $3 or $4 per oz, it depends on who you buy from. So in essence, smart buying goes something like this:

Current silver spot = $33. Buy now, or silver drops to $31. A person buys 10 oz of silver for $310 + price over spot = $3 per oz = $30 for 10oz = total cost of $340. You wait to see if silver goes up, let's say it jumps to $37/oz over the next 5-7 months. That means you have 10/oz of silver you paid $340, that would now be worth $370 + whatever price over spot you wanted to charge someone. Now this is just a small scale investing pattern so the profit doesn't seem that much, but when you start investing in a larger scale, the profit (along with the risk) also goes up. To be fair though, no one can accurately predict whether spot will go up or not, so that's why its a risk instead of a sure thing. Proceed with caution and don't invest anything you can't afford to lose. I started small, and just added on when I could afford to.
