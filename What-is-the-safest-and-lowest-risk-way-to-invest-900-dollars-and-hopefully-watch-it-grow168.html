US Treasuries are the "safest" but the return is nearly zero these days. TIPS (Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities) are a special - their interest rate is a fixed rate plus the current inflation rate. The minimum purchase amount is $100.

Next in safety is CD's from a FDIC insured bank. The interest rate is a little higher but your investment is also protected by the US gov't. Normally the min amount is $1000.

A savings account at a FDIC insured bank is also protected by the US gov't. The min amount is usually $25 or so.

Next would be to invest in a highly rated bond fund. Min for a mutual fund would be $3000 or so. This is the first investment that I have mentioned in which you could lose money.
