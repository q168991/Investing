Hi, stocks are basically shares in a company. If a company has a million shares of ten pounds each, then the value (market capitalisation) of the company = 10 X 1,000,000 = 10 millions pounds.

When you buy shares you are buying part of the worth of the company, usually a minute part eg 500 shares out of the million means you hold 500 divide by million = 0.05% of the company. Bigger investors eg directors may own 1/4 or 1/2 the company by holding 250,000 or 500, 000 shares, respectively, in this example.

The value os shares is mainly determined by the investor senfiment at a particular time which is basically supply and demand. When more people are buying, the price rises and vice versa.

Many factors determine whether people buy or sell. eg general financial state, the recommendations of analysts, reports of the company [whether good or bad], adverse markt conditions, interest rates [high interest rates mean people can get a decent rate in a building society, so they do not buy shares, therefore share prices fall: look out at news on Wed 31st and Thu 1st as there is a US Federal Reserve interest rate decision at about 6 pm on Wed], world catastrophes such as 9/11, etc.

Remember, the UK share market is a mirror of the US Dow Jones. If the Americans sneeze, UK gets a cold!!!

If you are seriously interested, locate a good broker first and test your strategies in a dummy account before risking any real cash. After a few weeks start off with small amounts, then gradually build as you gain experience.

You can either trade speculatively (day trading) (very risky, but can be highly lucrative) or invest for medium to long term.

Look up some sites with technical analysis facilities from graphs eg ***updata.co.uk AND ***igmarkets***, to learn about moving averages, Fiboncci, Gann index, Ichimoku, Bollinger, etc. You may need to open trial accounts without obligation.
