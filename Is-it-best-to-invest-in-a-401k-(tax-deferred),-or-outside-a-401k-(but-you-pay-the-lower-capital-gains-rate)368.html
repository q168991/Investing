The question is not easy and it depnds on what your investments are going to be and how you are going to handle those investments. There is no question that if you are going to buy an hold a mountain of growth stock for 30 years, you would almost surely be better off doing it outside your retirement funds so your gains are taxed as capital gains instead of income. The problem is that means you have serious trouble diversifying and balancing your portfolio without paying taxes unless your income is growing so that you can diversify and balance with new purchases.
